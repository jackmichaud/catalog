Native plants, such as the Flowering Dogwood (Virginia's state tree and flower), have evolved in this region for thousands of years. They provide essential food and shelter for local birds, insects, and wildlife that non-native plants cannot support.

While exploring, please be aware of these common hazardous plants:

üö´ Poison Ivy (Toxicodendron radicans)

"Leaves of three, let it be."

Look for clusters of three leaflets with a glossy surface. All parts of the plant contain urushiol, which causes an itchy rash.

üö´ English Ivy (Invasive)

While not poisonous to touch, this invasive vine climbs trees and blocks sunlight, eventually killing the tree. It is a major threat to Charlottesville's canopy.
